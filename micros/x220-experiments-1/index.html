480 Words

I decided to learn a little more about computer hardware by upgrading my latop. I bought a second one for parts/testing.

Quick specs (original):

Purchase date: 2016 (second hand)
Cost: €300
Battery life (playing youtube on firefox, ubuntu 20.04): 76 minutes
Battery type: 9 cell
RAM: 8GB
MEM: 150GB SSD
CPU: Intel Core i5-2520M (2.5-3.2 GHz, 3MB L3 Cache)
CPUS: 4
GPU: Intel HD Graphics 3000

Note on battery life: this laptop has the extended 9-cell battery which was excellent until recently. I’m not sure if there was something specific that damaged it. It went from several hours to the above. Also worth noting is that its self-assessment was wildly innacurate, it said it had less than ten percent left after 10m, but in fact this was about a tenth of the way through. Also I realised after an hour that the screen brightness was low which might make a difference, but since the biggest issue was obviously the battery self-assessment it doesn’t matter too much.

Quick specs (new 1):

Purchase date: 09.12.2020
Cost: €150
Battery life (playing youtube on firefox, ubuntu 20.04): 112 minutes
Battery type: 6 cell
RAM: 4GB
MEM: 300GB HDD
CPU: Intel Core i5-2520M (2.5-3.2 GHz, 3MB L3 Cache)
CPUS: 4
GPU: Intel HD Graphics 3000

The battery on this one is better. Not only does it know what is going on (showed consistent times throughout), it also lasts longer. After swapping the battery packs, it is clear that the first one is faulty, not the laptop. Thank god for a test machine.

From what I read, I can get 16GB of RAM, and I’ll change the SSD and the battery and see if I can get a new one for cheaper than my old one with better specs across the board.

I’m choosing to do this on the x220 since it is the most popular hacktop, so there is loads of info about what works and what doesn’t. And the good news is, there are versions with better chips (though they are more expensive) so even when I have fully upgraded my one, it will still be possible to go further. Hopefully this way I can survive until a new generation of decent machines comes out (with smartphone chips, a la the new macbook).

Final note: the new one is matt black, the other was shiny. And the keyboards have a different touch.

New commands:

lscpu																																											# cpu overview
cat /proc/cpuinfo													    				# Good place to go for cpu info
sudo lshw -C display                # For gpu info
sudo lsblk -d -o name,rota      # lsblk is great for when you are meddling with mounts and disks, but this was especially cool-- it finds the 'rotatability' of all devices, so you can check if you have an ssd or an hdd. This also found the dvd reader on my new dockingstation!

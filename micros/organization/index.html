I have (once again) spent some time organizing and archiving my digital detritus. Here is how things are at the moment:

  • increasingly, all personal writings are in a private git repo. Whenever I come across old caches of writing I dump it into a hidden folder, and if it is really embarassing I also delete it so it only exists in a git blob, making the chance of running across it again by chance almost zero.
  • as much of my personal computer setup as possible in public git repos, meaning they are available much more widely and securely (don’t need to put my password into a strange pc to get my dotfiles), and more robust against accidental deletion. (There are mutliple actors backup up public git repos.)
  • I have started a decentralized digital library with some friends using syncthing. All my digital books will end up there.
  • Photos in google photos.

I have two old cloud storage spaces, both google drive accounts, which after repeated purgings of everything in the above categories (aka all text) are now under 10GB each, mostly videos that are too long to go in with my photos.

Is that it? No hard drives, pen drives, old hdds under the stairs? Well there is also my collection of SD cards, lets look at those (these are to some degree work tools too, hence the number), from largest to smallest:

  • 256GB SD: my ‘big external hard drive’, nothing on it but Pasolini movies at the moment, I intent to fill it with films
  • 128GB SD: 108GB of roms and a retropi
  • 128GB SD: 108GB of roms and a retropi (I had three at one point, but christmas)
  • 128GB SD: ‘sound server’ this was my raspi music machine, plugged into speakers, though it only has a could of GB of music at the moment (mostly Japanese City Pop).
  • 32GB SD: operating system for a jetson nano, plus kodi (I tried using the jetson nano as a media center before I realised it doesn’t have a soundcard)
  • 32GB SD: probably another test raspi or jetson setup, raspi lights don’t flash properly when I put it in though
  • 16GB SD: raspi setup of unknown state and provinence. Didn’t look very interesting last time I checked.

What else, who has escaped the dragnet? There is the 500GB SSD that has my computer OS in it, I’m taking it back to Vienna (I game my brother my spare laptop), but that hardly counts, you are allowed to have a disk in your computer (mine just happens to be in my pocket). There are also a collection of pdfs in a zotero account that I think I can get out somehow, and no doubt some more caches will show up at some point.

My aim is generally to keep things as minimal as possible while avoiding overdependence on third-parties and avoiding hard drives. In practice this means not really having anything nailed down. If all my computers were stolen/destroyed tomorrow I wouldn’t blink an eye.

What would I miss most in the world? Probably my photos are my most fragile collection funnily enough, which is strange because I have never cared for photos. But they are all stored in one fairly brittle location, basically at the benevolence of google.

I continue to love git of course. If I ever felt the need to back up all my writing, I would just copy one folder (.git) someplace and be done. Loverly.

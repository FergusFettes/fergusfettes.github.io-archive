Fergus Fettes/Book Reviews 2021

Created Sun, 18 Apr 2021 17:45:27 +0100
518 Words

I have started reading again. Wonderful.

I am going to disregard the shameful reading of 2020. 2020 I read Marginal Revolution, Hacker News, listened to podcasts, watched YouTube videos, and browsed instagram. But mostly I learned the trade of the software developer.

But now I am not so desperate on that front, and I am taking some steps slowly back to earlier ways, and I will read again. I have already started.

I started this year in forced lockdown in Scotland. On my way there I picked up a book by Svante Paabo from a friend in London, they must have been my first book of the year. It was lovely autobiographical tale of the invention of a new form of anthropology, based around sequencing tiny amounts of genetic information. Very nice.

Then in forced lockdown– did I read anything? Despite being trapped in a turret in my sisters house? I don’t think so. High stress. No chill.

Then I returned to vienna in March, and when I got back I spontaneously ready an Asimov book that I had bought with me– wonderful! I am suddently reminded that reading is easy, engaging, thrilling, fun!

And thats it. Around that time I also recieved in the mail a set of printed out blogs– from the LessWrong communty. A gateway drug back into books. And I bought Kim Stanley Robinsons Ministry for the Future. And I’m back in business. I feel like this will go on.

So, to the Ministry for the Future. I am very tempted to give it a perfect 5. It was a fantastic book, very moving, very stimulating, very encouraging. It makes me want to act. But it was also wonderfully humane, and optimistic. It was terrifying. The fact that it was optimistic, and yet paints so hard and complex a picture of how to overcome the climate disaster– this is deeply troubling. It will be such work. It will be an immense effort.

Yes I will give it a five. It did its job as well as could be expected I believe. I can’t imaging it being done better. I have no criticisms.

I will act in this century along the lines laid out by The Ministry for the Future. And LessWrong. And 80k. I’m not sure what I will do or how exactly I will take part. I’m not sure exactly the role I will have. But I am ready for anything, for everything. I am still skilling up and driving towards some destination.

It looks like I will take part in a little excursion with Enrique. He is organizing an Arts and Science conference in Jena. He would like me to be there are a general instrument of good feeling and tool for communication. I think this would suit me well, and may lead to good things. Already it has!

Anyway. I will go back to my books now. I will plow on with the third Foundation book, though I think the series jumped the shark the moment it became one. The first was very good, and would have worked as a standalone.

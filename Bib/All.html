Economic Growth

Again and again I see the idea that economic growth is good, that it drags people out of poverty, that it improves quality of life.

More importantly, it often seems like all the things that people want correlate with economic growth. So, even if you are unsatisfied with it as a metric for human wellbeing or happiness or anything else, finding another way of measuring what you ‘really want’ would be a waste of time because GDP already measures what you think you really want to measure. And there is already a tonne of research literature about it, so trying to create a new index and create a new body of literature on your new, improved index would be a huge waste of time.

Of course if you can invent a wellbeing index that doesn’t correlate strongly with GDP then you will have something significant. But such a thing doesn’t seem to exist.

Here is a good article that covers a lot of this stuff quite nicely: Economic Growth in 5 Figures.

There are also various things from eg. Our World in Data that cover this.

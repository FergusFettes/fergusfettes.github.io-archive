“Salamanders have their appointed Dwelling in Fire;
Sylphs in the Air; Nymphs in the flowing Waters;
and Gnomes in Earthen-burrows, but the creature whose
substance is Bliss is everywhere at home. All sounds,
even to the roaring of Lions, the screeching of the
nightly Owls, the laments and groans of those entrapped
in Hell, are as sweet Musick to her. All odours, even
to the foulest stench of Corruption, are to her as the
delight of roses and Lilies. All savours, even to the
banquet-table of the Harpys of heathen lore, are as Sweet
loaves and spiced Ale.
Wandering at noon through the Waste-Places of the world,
it seems to her she is refreshed by Canopies of flocking
Angels.
The earnest seeker will look for her in All places,
however dim and sordid, of this world or in the seven
others. Thrust a keen Sword-blade through her and it will \
seem as a fountain of Divine and Pure pleasure. \
These eyes, by Translation, have been given to see her ways;
and an equal gift as revealed by Wisdom is sometimes granted
the Child.”

~ Jane Lead1
